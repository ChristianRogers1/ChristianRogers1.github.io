Research

Mechanistic Interpretability of Reward Functions

At the Aligned Robust Interactive Autonomy (ARIA) Lab, I investigate how reward functions within reinforcement learning models can be deconstructed to reveal the underlying decision-making processes. This research aims to build more interpretable AI systems by demystifying complex reward mechanisms.

Key Topics: Neural network interpretability, reinforcement learning, algorithm transparency.

AI in Stroke Rehabilitation

As a part-time research engineer, I contribute to projects that leverage machine learning and digital signal processing to optimize stroke rehabilitation. By analyzing biomedical signals and motion data, we develop novel treatments to improve patient's lives.

Key Topics: Biomedical signal processing, Corticospinal stimulation, ML Rehab Analysis.

Projects

DeepForge

A Work-In-Progress GUI+Library which allows for automated design, training, and deployment of AI models, as well as a built-in Neural Architecture Search, and easy conversion to-and-from PyTorch or Tensorflow.

ML-Lens

A GUI+Library which automated the mechanistic interpretability and visualization of newly trained models, as well as allowing pytorch extensions for new model development and new interpretability techniques. Compatible with DeepForge, PyTorch, Tensorflow, or JAX models.

MathML Explorer

An interactive platform that merges applied mathematics with machine learning concepts, aimed at helping fellow students and researchers understand complex computational theories.
