Enhancing Stroke Recovery Assessment: A Machine Learning Approach to Real-World Hand Function Analysis

Ukey et al.

2025

Overview

This work presents a machine learning approach to assessing stroke recovery through analysis of real-world hand function data. By leveraging sensor data collected in naturalistic settings rather than controlled clinical environments, we develop methods that provide a more accurate and comprehensive picture of a patient's functional recovery trajectory.

Key Contributions

  • Development of ML models for analyzing real-world hand function data from stroke survivors
  • Demonstration that real-world assessment provides complementary insights to standard clinical evaluations
  • A framework for continuous, remote monitoring of stroke recovery progress
  • Validation of the approach against established clinical outcome measures

Relevance

Traditional stroke recovery assessment relies on periodic clinical visits where patients perform standardized tasks in controlled settings. This approach captures only a snapshot of function and may not reflect how patients use their affected hand in daily life. Our machine learning approach analyzes data from wearable sensors during everyday activities, offering clinicians a richer, more ecologically valid view of recovery that can inform personalized treatment plans.

This research was conducted in collaboration with the Neural Plasticity, Rehabilitation & Movement Dynamics (NERD) Lab under Dr. Sethi at the University of Utah.
